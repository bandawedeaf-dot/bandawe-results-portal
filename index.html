About Bandawe School for the Deaf

Our History

Bandawe School for the Deaf was established on 25th April, 2005, as a unit based in Bandawe Primary School to provide quality education to learners with hearing impairment. Located at Bandawe near Chintheche, Nkhata Bay, Northern Malawi, the school serves as a center of excellence for deaf education in Malawi. The CCAP Synod of Livingstonia is the proprietor of the school. The school receives its financial and material support from the Government of Malawi through the district council and well-wishers.

Since its founding, the school has grown to accommodate learners from pre-school and Standard 1 to Standard 8, offering the Malawian National Curriculum in academics and the following vocational skills: Carpentry, Tailoring, Bicycle Repairing and Maintenance, and Animal Husbandry.

Our Mission

To educate learners with hearing impairment to achieve high moral, spiritual, social and economic standards through quality academics and practical vocational skills, empowering them to become independent and productive citizens.

Our Vision

A society where every person with hearing impairment has equal access to quality education and essential skills for a brighter independent future.
